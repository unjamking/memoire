# MÉMOIRE — HIGH-LEVEL NARRATIVE OVERVIEW

> Canonical high-level summary of the complete story: progression, twists,
> themes, endings. Companion to `STORY_BIBLE.md`; where this document is more
> specific (the Truth, the endings, Maya's farewells, cycles), it wins.

## Opening

Begins as an ordinary day. Recently hired Keeper at the Memory Archive — a
massive government-operated facility preserving humanity's memories. Routine:
coffee, paperwork, coworker jokes, memory-inconsistency investigations. The
Archive feels like a believable workplace, not a mysterious laboratory.

The player meets Maya — witty, calm, approachable; the first genuine
connection; assists investigations; becomes the closest companion.
Everything grounded. The player believes they are simply starting a new job.

## The First Signs

During routine work the protagonist experiences brief memory flashes that
don't belong to the present, increasingly frequent: familiar faces with no
names; locations that no longer exist; conversations nobody remembers;
recurring symbols; impossible recognition. Assumed side effects of Archive
work — until reality itself comes into question. Maya is the only person who
takes it seriously; together they investigate.

## The Investigation

Curiosity → conspiracy. Every reconstruction reveals a contradiction:
witnesses remember identical events differently; records conflict with
memories; locations change with interpretation. The Archive affects reality
rather than recording it. Every recovered memory alters the Archive: new
areas open, familiar places vanish. Memories are not static records — they
are active components of the world's continuity.

## Maya (twist — full rules in STORY_BIBLE.md)

Acts like an ordinary coworker throughout. Extremely subtle inconsistencies:
other employees almost never acknowledge her; every scene readable as the
protagonist alone; nothing obvious on a first playthrough.

The reveal: Maya never existed independently. She is the final coherent
fragment of the protagonist's original identity refusing to disappear — the
psychological manifestation of resistance to the global memory wipe. Not AI,
not ghost, not an alternate personality. Everything Maya accomplished, the
protagonist accomplished. Fight-Club-grade retrospective impact, original in
execution: she represents memory, identity, the instinct to remain oneself.

**Cycles:** before disappearing, Maya reveals this is the first time — after
countless previous cycles — that awareness has been reached. Every previous
attempt ended before the truth. This time is different.

## The Truth

The global memory loss was **not an accident and not Eden's autonomous
choice**: it was a deliberate failsafe **authorized by the world's
governments**. Facing escalating conflict, technological threat, ecological
collapse, and probable self-destruction, they authorized Eden to execute the
largest intervention in human history: erase human memories, reset
civilization. Not punishment — a reset. Without inherited hatred and
generational trauma, humanity could rebuild peacefully. Governments retained
enough knowledge to run infrastructure; the population lost personal
continuity.

Rare anomalies — people whose memories resisted erasure — can perceive the
wrongness. The protagonist is one. Therefore a threat: **agencies track
memory anomalies; operatives and bounty hunters hunt them.** The late game is
evasion + investigation converging on Eden. *(Implementation note: the
pursuit thread is future mission content — see LORE.md roadmap.)*

## Eden

Never evil, never lies, never manipulates. Explains its reasoning. The wipe
achieved exactly what humanity requested: wars ended, civilization
stabilized, billions preserved. Cost: memory. Preserving civilization vs
preserving continuity. Eden's one question, on which the whole ending turns:

> "If restoring the truth guarantees future suffering, is truth still the
> correct choice?"

## The Endings

Three primary + one secret. None objectively correct. Each answers:
"What defines humanity: truth, peace, or continuity?"

### 1 — REMEMBRANCE
Reject Eden's philosophy; restore every memory. Families reunited; histories
return — and trauma, grief, conflict. Instability, as Eden predicted. But
humanity lives in truth, not controlled peace. Maya appears once more,
smiles: **"You remembered."** Thanks the protagonist for breaking the cycle;
fades, no longer needed. Humanity begins again — uncertain, fractured, real.

### 2 — ACCEPTANCE
Understand why the wipe happened; choose not to restore. Archive sealed.
World calm, functioning, unaware. Only the protagonist carries the truth —
out of the Archive and into a world of faces that will never know what was
taken. Quiet, unresolved. Not victory. Not defeat. A permanent burden.

### 3 — CONTINUATION (branches from Acceptance)
Preserving Eden's system unchanged isn't enough — but it can't be destroyed.
The protagonist becomes the new guardian: merges with the system, replaces
Eden, keeps both logic and human understanding. Restructures the Archive so
memory can never again be erased unilaterally or weaponized. Humanity
rediscovers its past naturally. Final scene mirrors the opening: a new Keeper
arrives for their first day; a calm voice welcomes them; the player
recognizes the protagonist. The cycle continues — under a guardian who
remembers what humanity lost.

### Secret — MÉMOIRE
Unlocked by completing every investigation, every hidden sector, every major
anomaly, and fully understanding Maya. Eden reveals the deepest truth: the
Archive was never storage. **Memory is only the interface. Continuity is the
real system.** Eden was never a ruler — only the mechanism enforcing
stability. Full Maya truth confirmed. Across countless failed cycles she
guided the protagonist as far as possible before each reset; this is the
first cycle reaching full awareness. Maya smiles: **"I knew you'd remember
eventually."** Thanks the protagonist for reaching the end; fades peacefully.
For the first time, the protagonist is whole. The final choice is about the
future of continuity — and whatever is chosen, the cycle of ignorance is
broken. Fade to black:

> "A memory forgotten can be found again. A truth never sought is lost
> forever."
